Editorial Workflow

AI-assisted drafting with rigorous human review

AgriCalc Pakistan uses AI to support drafting and structure while keeping human editorial review at the center of every published article.

AI writing principles

AI should support the process without compromising quality or trust.

Original drafts

Content should be written to answer a real need in a fresh and useful way.

Clear language

Technical terms should be explained so the content remains accessible to farmers, students, and professionals.

Evidence-based

AI should never invent facts, statistics, quotations, or field recommendations.

Editorially aligned

Drafts must follow the site’s tone, formatting rules, and structure before publication.

Article creation workflow

Each article follows a consistent process from research to final review.

  1. Research the topic and gather reliable sources.
  2. Create an outline based on the user intent and article type.
  3. Draft the content in clear, practical language.
  4. Fact-check claims, dates, and recommendations.
  5. Complete editorial review for readability and structure.
  6. Complete SEO review for metadata, headings, and links.
  7. Complete accessibility review for headings, alt text, and navigation.
  8. Review grammar and consistency.
  9. Receive final approval before publication.
  10. Publish, monitor engagement, and update as needed.

Standard article prompt

Every article should be built around a clear structure and user outcome.

  • Title that reflects the main topic and search intent.
  • SEO title optimized for clarity and relevance.
  • Meta description that previews the page accurately.
  • Slug that supports readability and clean URL structure.
  • Short summary to explain the value immediately.
  • Introduction that frames the problem and promise.
  • Table of contents for long-form readability.
  • Main content with practical sections and examples.
  • Tips and warnings where useful or necessary.
  • FAQ covering common reader questions.
  • Summary that reinforces the core lesson.
  • Related articles and related calculators.

Template standards for core page types

These templates keep the site consistent as it grows.

Pillar page template

  • Overview of the main topic.
  • Clear navigation to supporting articles.
  • Topic summaries and practical categories.
  • Links to calculators, glossary terms, FAQs, and resources.

FAQ template

  • Clear question.
  • Direct answer.
  • Optional expanded explanation.
  • Internal links where they add value.

Glossary template

  • Term and definition.
  • Simple explanation for general readers.
  • Related articles and calculators.
  • Category label for organization.

Calculator explanation template

  • Purpose and what the tool helps calculate.
  • Inputs and units.
  • High-level calculation method.
  • How to use the tool and how to interpret the result.
  • FAQ and related educational guides.

Image and style guidelines

Visual content should support understanding, not distract from it.

  • Use relevant illustrations, diagrams, and original photographs where possible.
  • Optimize images for page speed and accessibility.
  • Use descriptive alt text and meaningful filenames.
  • Avoid decorative images that add no clear value.
  • Maintain consistent terminology, formatting, capitalization, and units across the site.

Human review checklist

Before publishing, every page should be checked for quality and trustworthiness.

  • Accuracy of statements and recommendations.
  • Grammar, spelling, and readability.
  • Internal links and external references.
  • Metadata, structured data, and page titles.
  • Accessibility and keyboard-friendly interactions.
  • No broken links, placeholders, or duplicate metadata.

Publishing and maintenance standards

Good content should stay current and useful long after publication.

  • Review content periodically and update it when guidance changes.
  • Refresh metadata, internal links, and references when new content is added.
  • Monitor search visibility, engagement, and user feedback.
  • Keep the editorial system scalable so new authors, languages, and topics can be added without breaking consistency.

Frequently asked questions

Can AI be used to create articles?

Yes, but every draft must be reviewed by a human editor for accuracy, clarity, and alignment with the site’s standards.

What is the most important publishing rule?

Publish only content that is useful, accurate, and supported by a real review process.

How often should content be updated?

At least periodically, and sooner when field guidance, recommendations, or user needs change.
