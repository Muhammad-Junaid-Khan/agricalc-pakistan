Editorial System

Editorial system, templates, and knowledge base architecture

This framework gives AgriCalc Pakistan a repeatable system for publishing useful, accurate, and scalable agriculture content in English and future multilingual editions.

Core content categories

These categories form the base of the publishing system.

Crop Guides

Wheat, rice, cotton, maize, sugarcane, vegetables, fruits, pulses, and oilseed crops.

Crop Protection

Fertilizer, pesticide, herbicide, and fungicide guides with practical field advice.

Soil & Water

Irrigation, soil health, water management, weather, and solar agriculture content.

Farm Systems

Machinery, livestock, dairy, poultry, organic farming, modern farming, and farm business.

Technology & Tools

Agricultural technology, calculators, checklists, and glossary-based reference content.

Policy & Programs

Government schemes and public agricultural programs for farmers and advisors.

Article types supported

The system supports a wide range of article formats without changing the underlying structure.

  • Beginner guides
  • Advanced guides
  • How-to guides
  • Problem and solution articles
  • Comparison articles
  • Seasonal guides
  • Buying guides
  • Checklists
  • Reference articles
  • Glossary pages
  • FAQ pages
  • Calculator guides

Standard article template

Every article follows the same structure so content remains consistent and easy to scale.

  1. Title that matches the user intent.
  2. Short summary to explain the value quickly.
  3. Featured image with descriptive alt text.
  4. Table of contents for long-form readability.
  5. Introduction that explains the problem and promise.
  6. Main sections with clear subheadings and examples.
  7. Practical tips that improve usability.
  8. Common mistakes to prevent avoidable errors.
  9. Warnings where health, safety, or cost risks apply.
  10. FAQ section with genuine user questions.
  11. Summary that reinforces the main lesson.
  12. Related articles and a related calculator.
  13. References where appropriate.

Writing and formatting rules

The editorial system is built for readability and practical understanding.

  • Use simple, professional, and practical language.
  • Avoid unnecessary jargon; explain technical terms when needed.
  • Use short paragraphs, bullets, tables, callout boxes, and examples.
  • Keep content useful for farmers, students, advisors, and professionals.
  • Write for people first, then optimize for search.

Knowledge base architecture

This model keeps content organized as the site grows.

  • Pillar pages cover broad topics and act as hubs.
  • Supporting guides answer detailed questions beneath each pillar.
  • FAQ pages capture common user questions and search intent.
  • Calculator guides connect educational content with practical calculations.
  • Glossary pages explain terms and improve knowledge depth.
  • Reference articles support repeat visits and long-tail search traffic.

Review workflow

Every page is reviewed before publication and updated as new information becomes available.

  1. Check grammar and readability.
  2. Verify facts and remove unsupported claims.
  3. Confirm internal links and calculator references.
  4. Optimize images and alt text.
  5. Review metadata and structured data.
  6. Publish, monitor, and update as needed.

AI-assisted publishing rules

AI can assist with drafting, but human review remains mandatory.

  • Review every draft manually.
  • Fact-check before publication.
  • Edit for originality, clarity, and usefulness.
  • Improve language and structure where needed.
  • Do not publish unchecked AI-generated content.
